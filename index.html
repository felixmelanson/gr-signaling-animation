GR Signaling Animation

An interactive HTML canvas animation of glucocorticoid receptor (GR) signaling, built for a PHAR 370 presentation at Queen's University.

Cytoplasmic activation: resting chaperone complex → prednisolone binding → FKBP51/52 switch → dynein-dynactin recruitment → retrograde microtubule transport → nuclear pore entry → HSP90 dissociation

Genomic signaling: homodimerization → transactivation (GRE binding, DUSP1 induction) → transrepression (NF-κB/AP-1 tethering)

Additional Note (GRdim hypothesis): An experiment using a KO mouse model engineered to abolish GR dimerization; the goal was to test whether transrepression could be isolated from transactivation, retaining anti-inflammatory efficacy while eliminating metabolic side effects. The experiment failed: without dimerization, DUSP1 induction is lost and the anti-inflammatory effect collapses entirely, demonstrating that the two pathways are functionally inseparable. This has direct clinical implications because transactivation cannot be eliminated without losing therapeutic efficacy, glucocorticoid-driven gene expression in metabolic tissues is unavoidable. Adverse effects therefore concentrate in tissues with the highest GR expression: liver, skeletal muscle, adipose tissue, and pancreas.
